I am currently an assistant professor in the Department of Statistics, at Rutgers University. Before that, I was a postdoctoral scholar at the University of Chicago Data Science Institute, advised by Prof. Rina Foygel Barber. I received my Ph.D. from the Department of Statistics, University of Wisconsin-Madison under the supervision of Prof. Anru Zhang in 2022. I recevied my Bachelor's degree in Statistics from Renmin University of China in 2017.



You can also find me on Google Scholar.

Research Interest

I am broadly interested in methodology, theory and computation of high dimensional statistical problems. Specifically, currently I am interested in:

  • High dimensional statistical inference
  • Distribution-free inference
  • Robust statistics
  • Tensor learning
  • Computational complexity of statistical inference
